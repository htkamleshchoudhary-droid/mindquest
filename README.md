Stage 3 – Social Confidence Practice
A friendly character appears and guides the player.
Example dialogue:
Character:
“Let’s practice some real-life conversations. Don’t worry if you make mistakes. Just take a deep breath and speak slowly.”
The player turns on the microphone/speaker and answers.

The 6 Conversation Scenarios

1. Giving aCompliment
Character says:

“Let’s practice giving a compliment. Imagine your friend did a great presentation. What would you say?”
Player speaks.
Example responses:
“You explained that really well.”
“Your presentation was very clear.”
Character feedback:
“Nice! Compliments make people feel appreciated.”

2. Introducing Yourself
Character:
“Now let’s practice introducing yourself to someone new.”
Player speaks.
Example: “Hi, I’m Mia. I like drawing and photography.”
Feedback: “Great! Saying your name clearly helps people remember you.”

3. Asking for Directions
Character:
“Imagine you are in a new place and need directions. What would you say?”
Example response: “Excuse me, could you tell me where the library is?”
Feedback: “Very polite. Good job asking clearly.”

4. Joining a Group Conversation
Character:
“Some classmates are talking about a movie you watched. How would you join the conversation?”
Example: “I watched that movie too! It was really good.”
Feedback: “Nice! Adding your opinion helps you join the group.”

5. Asking for Help
Character:
“You didn’t understand a homework question. What would you say to your teacher?”
Example: “Excuse me, could you explain this question again?”
Feedback: “Good! Asking for help is a strong skill.”

6. Saying No Politely
Character:
“Your friend asks you to skip class. What would you say?”
Example: “I can’t today, I have an important class.”
Feedback: “Great! You said no in a respectful way.”

Small Feature That Will Make This Stage Better
Before each response, the character says:
“Take a deep breath… speak slowly… you’ve got this.”
This helps players feel calmer before speaking.

How the Game Responds
The game doesn’t judge harshly. Instead it gives encouraging feedback, like:
“That sounded confident!”
“Good start, try speaking a little louder.”
“Nice job explaining clearly.”
This makes the game feel supportive instead of stressful.

Stage 4 – Focus & Attention Training
When the player chooses this option, the character appears again.
Character dialogue example:
“Let’s train your focus. Stay calm and pay attention to the details.”

1. Firefly Focus Game
Goal: Improve selective attention.
Game idea: Fireflies appear on the screen.
Rules:
Tap only the glowing blue fireflies
Ignore the yellow and red ones.
If the player taps the wrong one, the game gently says: “Focus on the blue ones.”
Why it works This trains the brain to filter distractions.
Background
Dark night forest
Calm music
Soft glowing lights

2. Falling Leaves Game
Goal: Improve visual focus.
Leaves fall slowly from the top of the screen.
Example rule: “Catch only the green leaves.”
Other leaves fall too:
red
yellow
brown
Player must pay attention and choose carefully.
Why it works This improves visual attention and decision making.
Environment
Soft forest colors
Relaxing wind sounds
